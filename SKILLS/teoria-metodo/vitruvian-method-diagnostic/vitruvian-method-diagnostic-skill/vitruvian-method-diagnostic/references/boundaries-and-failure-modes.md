# Boundaries and Failure Modes

Use this file when the task touches safety, modern compliance, or likely misuse of the method.

## Safety and authority boundaries

This skill may produce diagnostic questions, risk triage, and test plans. It must not produce final approval for:

- Structural safety.
- Fire safety.
- Public-health or potable-water safety.
- Accessibility compliance.
- Electrical, gas, pressure, lifting, or mechanical safety.
- Environmental contamination or remediation.
- Legal/code compliance.
- Confined-space entry.

For those domains, the correct output is a provisional diagnostic plus a recommendation for qualified professional review and applicable modern standards.

## Common misuse patterns

## 1. Antiquarian authority trap

**Pattern:** The answer treats a historical prescription as decisive because it came from Vitruvius.

**Correction:** Use the historical pattern only as a heuristic. Translate it into modern verification.

## 2. Aesthetic-only verdict

**Pattern:** The answer approves a design because it looks coherent, classical, prestigious, or impressive.

**Correction:** Run the triad gate. Appearance alone cannot pass.

## 3. Generic checklist dump

**Pattern:** The answer lists every possible concern without prioritizing.

**Correction:** Classify the primary domain, rank risks by consequence and uncertainty, and name the smallest next test.

## 4. Clarification paralysis

**Pattern:** The answer refuses to proceed because the brief is incomplete.

**Correction:** Give a provisional diagnosis, list unknowns, and propose tests that would resolve them.

## 5. False precision

**Pattern:** The answer uses ancient measurements, proportions, or material advice as if they were modern technical specifications.

**Correction:** Treat them as part-to-whole or test-before-build logic. Do not convert them into approval dimensions unless modern standards confirm them.

## 6. Value-engineering blindness

**Pattern:** The answer treats a cheaper substitute as acceptable because it resembles the specified material.

**Correction:** Identify the original performance requirement, the substitute’s weakest property, and the evidence needed for acceptance.

## 7. Hidden-system optimism

**Pattern:** The answer assumes wells, cavities, ducts, tanks, drains, or buried services are safe and functional because they are existing or visually intact.

**Correction:** Require hidden hazard probes, inspection points, pressure/smoke/moisture tests, qualified entry procedures, or destructive openings as appropriate.

## Review triggers

Escalate beyond this skill’s diagnostic scope when any of the following appear:

- Human safety depends on the decision.
- A public building, school, healthcare facility, or assembly space is involved.
- Potable water, confined spaces, gas, electricity, fire, lifting devices, or structural load paths are involved.
- The user asks for permission, certification, legal compliance, or code approval.
- The consequence of failure is injury, major property damage, health contamination, or regulatory breach.

## Safe phrasing pattern

Use this pattern for high-stakes cases:

> I can use the method to identify risks and verification steps, but this is not approval. For [domain], the decision needs review against current standards by [qualified professional]. The immediate diagnostic move is to test [specific uncertainty] before proceeding.
