# Domain Checklists

Use these checklists to adapt the core method to common task types. Keep the user-facing answer concise; select only the relevant items.

## Site or plot selection

Check:
- Ground, drainage, flood risk, contamination, slope, erosion, and subsurface uncertainty.
- Sun, wind, humidity, heat island, freeze-thaw, salt, dust, or other climate loads.
- Water availability, discharge routes, utilities, access, emergency access, logistics, and future expansion.
- Health, comfort, noise, air quality, adjacency conflicts, and public/private relationship.
- Cost of remediation versus benefits of the location.

Typical verdict logic:
- **Proceed** if risks are understood and proportionate.
- **Test first** if ground, water, contamination, or climate data is missing.
- **Revise/reject** if the location creates persistent health, safety, access, or maintenance failures that the project cannot afford to solve.

## Facade or envelope review

Check:
- Orientation-specific exposure: rain, sun, wind, salt, pollution, impact, freeze-thaw, and thermal movement.
- Assembly logic: substrate, cavity, fixings, joints, fire barriers, waterproofing, vapor control, drainage, and ventilation.
- Material proof: tested samples, mockups, warranties, maintenance, cleaning, replacement, and installer familiarity.
- Module and tolerances: panel sizes, joint rhythm, corners, penetrations, openings, and termination details.
- Use and legibility: entry clarity, human scale, public image, coherence with structure and program.

Typical verdict logic:
- **Test first** for unproven substitutions, unusual exposures, or unverified fixings.
- **Revise** if the facade is visually strong but assembly logic is weak.
- **Reject** if it cannot drain, be inspected, meet safety requirements, or be maintained.

## Material substitution or value engineering

Check:
- Original performance reason: durability, fire, moisture, structural role, finish quality, warranty, module, or availability.
- Substitute performance: what is weaker, stronger, unknown, or dependent on installation quality.
- Exposure match: whether the substitute has evidence in the same climate, use, and assembly.
- Lifecycle cost: maintenance, replacement, cleaning, access, and failure consequence.
- Verification: mockup, sample test, supplier warranty, independent test data, installer review, and code/professional review where needed.

Typical verdict logic:
- **Proceed** only when the substitute preserves critical performance.
- **Test first** when claims are plausible but unproven.
- **Reject** when savings come from removing a critical property.

## Water source or services

Check:
- Intended use: potable, irrigation, process, fire, rainwater, greywater, drainage, or decorative water.
- Source quality, legal use, contamination risk, seasonal reliability, flow, pressure, storage, treatment, and monitoring.
- Distribution route, access, isolation, cleanouts, inspection points, overflow, backflow prevention, and failure consequences.
- Confined-space and hidden hazards before wells, tanks, ducts, trenches, or voids are entered.

Typical verdict logic:
- **Test first** by default unless current qualified data exists.
- **Proceed** only after quality, capacity, legal constraints, and maintenance are proven.
- **Reject** if a health-critical source cannot be verified or protected.

## Public building, civic space, or institutional brief

Check:
- Public role, dignity, legibility, access, security, queuing, gathering, and wayfinding.
- Fit between symbolic expression and actual function.
- Circulation hierarchy: public, staff, service, emergency, accessible, and ceremonial routes.
- Durability, maintenance, vandalism, cleaning, acoustic comfort, climate comfort, and operating cost.
- Whether form, structure, and program reinforce each other.

Typical verdict logic:
- **Revise** if symbolic appearance outruns circulation, access, or durability.
- **Test first** if user flows, capacity, or operating model are unproven.

## Private house, workplace, or hospitality plan

Check:
- Daily routines, privacy gradient, service routes, storage, daylight, comfort, views, and acoustic separation.
- Climate adaptation by room type and orientation.
- Social/status expectations versus practical operation and budget.
- Maintenance capacity and replacement cycles.

Typical verdict logic:
- **Revise** if spaces are organized by prestige rather than routine use.
- **Test first** if comfort, daylight, or circulation assumptions are unverified.

## Machine, moving assembly, or constructability system

Check:
- Load, center of gravity, support reactions, lever arms, rotation, friction, counterweight, ratio, and braking.
- Material capacity, fixings, wear points, access, failure mode, overload behavior, and user safety.
- Prototype or controlled test before live use.
- Inspection and maintenance after commissioning.

Typical verdict logic:
- **Test first** for any uncalculated load or unproven mechanism.
- **Reject** if no safe stop, support, or failure containment exists.
