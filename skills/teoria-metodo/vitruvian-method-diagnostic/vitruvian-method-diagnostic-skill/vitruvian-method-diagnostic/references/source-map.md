# Source-to-Method Map

This file explains how the skill was distilled from the Vitruvian source tradition. Use it only when the user asks about provenance, extraction logic, or why a move belongs in the skill.

The skill intentionally does not bundle the full source text. That keeps the skill procedural and prevents it from behaving like a historical reference tool.

## Extraction principle

A passage or theme was included only when it behaved like a repeatable operation:

1. It asks the practitioner to inspect conditions before designing.
2. It gives a test, sign, ordering principle, or decision criterion.
3. It recurs across domains rather than applying to one isolated antiquarian case.
4. It can be translated into a modern diagnostic equivalent without pretending ancient standards are sufficient today.

## High-yield source regions

## Book I: scope, adequacy, sites, orientation, and public works

Procedural yield:
- Architecture is judged by multiple adequacy criteria, not appearance alone.
- Site and climate analysis precede building form.
- Public works are classified by use and civic role.
- Orientation, exposure, access, and health are diagnostic inputs.

Modern method moves:
- Triad gate.
- Site-before-form.
- Environmental adaptation.
- Public/private use classification.

## Book II: origins, materials, and local availability

Procedural yield:
- Building methods arise from available means and environmental need.
- Materials differ by origin, preparation, and performance.
- Local conditions affect what is feasible and durable.

Modern method moves:
- Local means discipline.
- Material sample test.
- Substitution discipline.

## Books III-IV: proportion, temples, orders, and part-whole logic

Procedural yield:
- Systems are organized by modules and proportional relationships.
- Formal elements should belong to a coherent system.
- Parts are judged by their relation to the whole, not as isolated ornaments.

Modern method moves:
- Module/coherence test.
- Decorum/coherence review.
- Part-to-whole consistency checks.

## Book V: public buildings, theaters, acoustics, and urban functions

Procedural yield:
- Different public functions require different spatial organizations.
- Performance effects such as sound, circulation, and visibility must be tested.
- Civic use shapes geometry and hierarchy.

Modern method moves:
- Use-specific distribution.
- Public-building checklist.
- Performance-based review.

## Book VI: private buildings, climate, status, and rooms

Procedural yield:
- Domestic space changes with climate, social role, occupation, and resources.
- Rooms should be oriented and distributed by use.
- A generic plan is weak when users and climate differ.

Modern method moves:
- Use-specific distribution.
- Environmental adaptation.
- Fit to user and means.

## Book VII: finishes, preparation, and material procedure

Procedural yield:
- Finish quality depends on substrate preparation, sequence, and material condition.
- Material performance needs direct verification, not only category names.
- Hidden preparation work determines visible success.

Modern method moves:
- Material sample test.
- Hidden condition review.
- Commissioning by failure points.

## Book VIII: water discovery, source judgment, and conveyance

Procedural yield:
- Source quality is tested before conveyance.
- Water systems require attention to route, gradient, containment, and failure points.
- Invisible or enclosed dangers require probing before entry or use.

Modern method moves:
- Water/source verification.
- Hidden hazard probe.
- Commissioning by failure points.

## Book IX: measurement, astronomy, and instruments

Procedural yield:
- Orientation and measurement depend on external cycles and reference systems.
- Instruments translate observation into repeatable procedure.

Modern method moves:
- Environmental measurement.
- Test-before-judgment.
- Reference-system awareness.

## Book X: machines, hoisting, movement, and military devices

Procedural yield:
- Machines are judged by force transmission, centers, rotations, ratios, and load paths.
- Performance depends on controlled movement and failure containment.
- Devices need commissioning and adjustment, not merely description.

Modern method moves:
- Mechanics by centers and ratios.
- Commissioning by failure points.
- Prototype/load testing.

## What was excluded

Excluded or minimized:
- Philology, biography, edition history, and Latin textual questions.
- Ancient measurements as authoritative modern dimensions.
- Prescriptive ancient public-health or structural advice as final approval.
- Mythological, historical, or ornamental passages that do not produce repeatable diagnostic action.

## Translation rule

Always translate from ancient procedure to modern equivalent:

- “Inspect the source” becomes water-quality, capacity, contamination, legal, and maintenance verification.
- “Choose by winds and health” becomes climate, air quality, comfort, hazard, and exposure review.
- “Use proportion/order” becomes module, grid, interface, tolerance, and system coherence review.
- “Test material signs” becomes sample, mockup, lab, exposure, compatibility, and installer review.
- “Analyze machines by wheels/levers” becomes load path, torque, center of gravity, ratio, restraint, and failure mode review.
